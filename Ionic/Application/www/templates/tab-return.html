Over the last 100 years the average return of the stock market has been around 11%, before expenses such as taxes, investment fees and inflation. This is known as the nominal return. After you have adjusted for these expenses you are left with your real return

On average inflation has been around 3% per year. So you can do a quick calculation by subtracting inflation from the nominal return to get an expected return of 7%. This isn't the actual formula to account for inflation, but it is close enough for our purpose.

There is no guarentee that the next 100 years will be anything like the last 100. Past performance does not predict future results. However, this is the best information available.

We have chosen a conservative return 5%. Feel free to be more or less conservative in your estimate.
